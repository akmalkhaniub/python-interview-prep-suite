# 05 - Senior Leadership & Communication

## Mentorship & Leadership

### 1. How do you mentor junior ML Engineers?
**Answer:**
- **Code Reviews:** Provide constructive feedback that explains *why* a change is needed.
- **Pair Programming:** Work together on complex architectural problems.
- **Goal Setting:** Help them identify technical areas they want to grow in (e.g., MLOps or Deep Learning).
- **Delegation:** Give them ownership of a specific component, not just small tickets.

### 2. How do you handle ambiguity in a new ML project?
**Answer:**
- **Clarification:** Ask business stakeholders for the "success metric" (not just "high accuracy").
- **Exploration:** Start with a Baseline (e.g., Logistic Regression) to understand data signals.
- **Communication:** Flag early if data quality is insufficient or if the problem is not solvable with current ML techniques.

### 3. What is a "Project Retrospective" (Post-mortem)?
**Answer:**
A meeting after a project ends (or fails) to discuss:
- What went well?
- What didn't?
- What are the action items for the next project?
Focus on *systems* and *processes*, not individual blame.

## Communication & Documentation

### 4. Importance of "Architecture Decision Records" (ADRs)?
**Answer:**
A short document that records a significant architectural decision (e.g., choosing PyTorch over TensorFlow), including:
- Context.
- Alternatives considered.
- Rationale for the choice.
- Consequences.
Crucial for asynchronous, remote teams.

### 5. How do you explain a complex model failure to non-technical stakeholders?
**Answer:**
- **Avoid Jargon:** Don't talk about "gradient clipping."
- **Use Analogies:** Explain it as a "blind spot" or a "new pattern the model hasn't seen before."
- **Focus on Action:** Explain what the impact is and how you are fixing it.

### 6. What is "ML Practice" at a company level?
**Answer:**
Establishing standards for:
- How models are logged.
- How research notebooks are organized.
- Which frameworks are supported.
- Security and ethical guidelines for AI.
As a Senior, you are expected to drive these standards.
