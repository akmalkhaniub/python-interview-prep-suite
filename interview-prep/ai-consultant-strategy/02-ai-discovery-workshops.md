# 02 - AI Discovery & Workshops

## Workshop Preparation

### 1. Who are the key stakeholders for an AI Discovery workshop?
**Answer:**
- **Sponsors:** C-suite (CTO, CDO) for budget and vision.
- **Subject Matter Experts (SMEs):** The people who know the day-to-day pain points.
- **Data Engineers:** To verify data availability.
- **Legal/Compliance:** To identify early risks.

### 2. How do you prepare a "Problem Statement" for AI?
**Answer:**
Avoid: "We need an LLM."
Instead: "Our customer support team spends 40% of their time answering the same 10 FAQs, leading to a 24-hour response delay. We need to automate these responses while maintaining a 90% satisfaction score."

## Workshop Execution

### 3. Explain the "Design Thinking" approach for AI.
**Answer:**
1. **Empathize:** Understand user frustration with current systems.
2. **Define:** Pinpoint the exact bottleneck where AI can help.
3. **Ideate:** Brainstorm multiple AI solutions (e.g., chatbot vs. search vs. automated summary).
4. **Prototype:** Wireframes or a "Wizard of Oz" PoC.

### 4. How do you handle "Scope Creep" during discovery?
**Answer:**
Use a **"Parking Lot"** for ideas that are interesting but outside the current project's core objectives. Reiterate the primary business goal and ROI focus.

### 5. What is the "Success Criteria" for a Pilot/PoC?
**Answer:**
It should be:
- **Measurable:** (e.g., 20% reduction in time).
- **Time-bound:** (e.g., within 4 weeks).
- **Technically Validated:** (e.g., model achieves >85% F1-score).
- **User Validated:** (e.g., positive feedback from 5 internal users).

### 6. How do you prioritize use cases at the end of a workshop?
**Answer:**
Perform a **"Dot Voting"** or **"Impact/Effort"** mapping exercise with all stakeholders to ensure alignment and collective ownership of the chosen path.
