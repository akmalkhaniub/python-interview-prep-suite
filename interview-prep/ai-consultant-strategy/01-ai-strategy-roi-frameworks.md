# 01 - AI Strategy & ROI Frameworks

## Strategic Framing

### 1. How do you assess the "AI Readiness" of an organization?
**Answer:**
Use the **People-Process-Data-Technology** framework:
- **Data:** Availability, quality, and accessibility of historical data.
- **People:** Skills gap, internal buy-in, and leadership support.
- **Process:** Are current workflows documented? Is there a culture of experimentation?
- **Technology:** Cloud infrastructure, existing stack compatibility.

### 2. What is the "AI Opportunity Matrix"?
**Answer:**
A $2 \times 2$ grid to prioritize use cases:
- **X-axis:** Feasibility (Data availability, technical complexity).
- **Y-axis:** Business Impact (Revenue growth, cost reduction, risk mitigation).
- **Goal:** Focus on "Quick Wins" (High impact, high feasibility) first to build momentum.

### 3. How do you calculate ROI for a Generative AI project?
**Answer:**
- **Cost Reduction:** Hours saved per employee (e.g., $Avg\_Salary \times Hours\_Saved$).
- **Efficiency Gain:** Reduction in Error Rate or Cycle Time.
- **Revenue Enhancement:** Improved conversion rates or new product capabilities.
- **Subtraction:** Subtract model costs (Tokens), infrastructure, and human-in-the-loop (HITL) costs.

## Use Case Identification

### 4. Explain the "Build vs. Buy vs. Partner" decision for AI.
**Answer:**
- **Buy (SaaS):** Good for generic tasks (e.g., meeting transcription). Fast, lower cost, but no competitive advantage.
- **Partner (API/Consultancy):** Good for complex but common tasks (e.g., custom search). Balances speed with customization.
- **Build (Custom ML):** Good for core business IP (e.g., proprietary fraud detection). High cost/time but provides maximum differentiation.

### 5. What are common pitfalls in AI Strategy?
**Answer:**
- **Technology-first approach:** Implementing AI because it's "cool" rather than solving a business problem.
- **Data Silos:** Models can't access the data they need.
- **Ignoring the Last Mile:** Building a great model but failing to integrate it into user workflows.
- **Underestimating Change Management:** Employees resisting the new tool.

### 6. How do you structure an AI Roadmap for a 12-month period?
**Answer:**
- **Months 1-3:** Discovery, Pilot/PoC of a "Quick Win."
- **Months 4-6:** Scaling the pilot, establishing Governance.
- **Months 7-12:** Rolling out additional use cases, continuous monitoring and optimization.
