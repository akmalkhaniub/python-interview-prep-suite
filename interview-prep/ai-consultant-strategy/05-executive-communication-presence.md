# 05 - Executive Communication & Storytelling

## Presentation Skills

### 1. How do you structure a 15-minute Executive AI briefing?
**Answer:**
- **The Hook (1 min):** Why does AI matter for *this* company's specific goals?
- **The Problem (2 mins):** The core pain point being addressed.
- **The Solution (3 mins):** High-level explanation of the AI tool (no technical jargon).
- **The Impact (4 mins):** ROI, efficiency gains, and competitive advantage.
- **The Ask/Next Steps (3 mins):** Budget, pilot approval, or resource allocation.
- **Q&A (2 mins).**

### 2. How do you handle "Tough Questions" about AI job replacement?
**Answer:**
Be honest but focus on **Augmentation**: "AI will automate the mundane parts of the job (data entry, basic search), allowing the team to focus on higher-value strategic work. We are planning a training program to help the team transition."

### 3. What is "Executive Presence"?
**Answer:**
A combination of confidence, clarity, and the ability to listen. It's about being the "calm expert" in the room who can translate complex tech into business outcomes.

## Written Communication

### 4. Tips for writing an "Executive Summary"?
**Answer:**
- One page maximum.
- **BLUF:** Bottom Line Up Front (the core recommendation).
- Use bullet points for readability.
- Focus on outcomes (money saved, time gained) rather than technical details (f1-score, latent space).

### 5. How do you document "Findings and Recommendations"?
**Answer:**
Use the **Situation-Complication-Resolution (SCR)** framework:
- **Situation:** Current state of the business.
- **Complication:** Why the current state is no longer sufficient.
- **Resolution:** How the proposed AI solution solves the complication.

### 6. Importance of "Visual Storytelling"?
**Answer:**
Use simple, clear charts. Avoid "Spaghetti" diagrams. Use mockups/wireframes to make the future state feel "real" to the executives.
