# 05: Product & Marketing Strategy

This role is about **Revenue**, not just **Code**. You are a "Product Engineer".

## 1. The MRR Mindset
Every line of code should either **Reduce Churn** or **Increase Acquisition**.
- **Onboarding:** Reducing friction to get users to their "Aha!" moment faster.
- **Retention:** Building features that make the product "sticky" (e.g., historical data, integrations).
- **Expansion:** Upselling users to higher tiers via usage-based billing.

## 2. Speed over Polish
- **MVP vs. MLP:** Minimum Viable Product vs. Minimum Lovable Product.
- **Technical Debt:** Making conscious decisions to "build it fast" now and "fix it later" once revenue justifies it.

## 3. A/B Testing & Data-Driven Decisions
- Using tools like **PostHog** or **Split.io** to test different AI prompts or UI layouts.
- Tracking **LTV** (Lifetime Value) and **CAC** (Customer Acquisition Cost).

## 4. Communicating Value
- Instead of saying "I optimized the database", say "I reduced page load time by 40%, which increased conversion rates on the landing page".
- Explaining AI limitations to non-technical stakeholders (e.g., "Hallucinations can happen, here's how we guard against them").

## 5. Potential Interview Questions
1. **The revenue share model is milestone-based. How do you prioritize features based on revenue impact?**
   - *Answer:* I use the RICE framework (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort) but weighted heavily towards "Impact on MRR". I focus on features that solve immediate customer pain points or open up new market segments.
2. **If you have a choice between a 1-week "hacky" fix and a 1-month "clean" rewrite, which do you choose?**
   - *Answer:* In an early-stage SaaS, the hacky fix. Speed to market and validating the feature with real users is more important. Once it generates revenue, the rewrite becomes an investment in stability.
3. **How do you handle "Ownership" if the marketing team wants a feature you think is technically unsound?**
   - *Answer:* I communicate the risks clearly (security, performance, cost). I suggest a "compromise" or an "MVP version" to test the hypothesis before committing heavy resources.
