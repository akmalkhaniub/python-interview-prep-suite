# 03: Recording Checklist

A great script can be ruined by bad audio or lighting. Follow these "Quick Wins" before you hit record.

## 🛠️ The Setup
- [ ] **Lighting:** Face a window or use a desk lamp behind your camera. Never have a bright window *behind* you (you will be a silhouette).
- [ ] **Audio:** Use a dedicated mic or your wired earbuds. Laptop mics often sound "echoey."
- [ ] **Background:** Keep it clean. A bookshelf or a plain wall is better than a messy room.
- [ ] **Camera Level:** Place your laptop/camera at **eye level**. Use books to prop it up if needed. Looking "down" at the camera feels less professional.

## 🎭 The Delivery
- [ ] **Eye Contact:** Look at the **camera lens**, not your own face on the screen.
- [ ] **Energy:** Smile at the beginning and end. Start with slightly more energy than you think you need; cameras tend to "drain" personality.
- [ ] **Speed:** Speak slower than usual. Nervousness makes people rush.
- [ ] **The 5-Second Rule:** Wait 5 seconds after you finish speaking before stopping the recording. This prevents the "cut off" look at the end.

## 📝 Final Polish
- [ ] **Video Title:** Rename the file to `[YourName]_AI_ML_Intro.mp4`.
- [ ] **Check the Link:** If uploading to Loom/Drive, make sure "Anyone with the link" can view it.
