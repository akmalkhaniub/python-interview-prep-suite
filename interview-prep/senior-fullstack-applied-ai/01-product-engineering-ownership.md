# 01 - Product Engineering & Ownership

## Product Mindset

### 1. What does "End-to-End Ownership" mean in a high-impact team?
**Answer:**
It means being responsible for the entire lifecycle of a feature:
- **Discovery:** Clarifying ambiguous requirements with stakeholders.
- **Design:** Planning the architecture, UX flows, and data models.
- **Development:** Writing clean, tested code across the stack.
- **Deployment:** Getting the code into production and monitoring its success.
- **Iteration:** Using data and feedback to improve the feature.

### 2. How do you handle "Ambiguous Requirements"?
**Answer:**
- **Proactive Questioning:** Asking stakeholders about the "Why" behind a request.
- **Prototyping:** Building a quick mock-up to validate assumptions early.
- **RFCs/Design Docs:** Writing down the proposed solution to align all parties before coding.
- **MVP Approach:** Defining the minimum set of features that provide value and shipping them fast.

### 3. Balancing "Speed" vs. "Long-term Quality"?
**Answer:**
- **Speed:** Use for validating new ideas (prototyping) and critical hotfixes.
- **Quality:** Mandatory for core data models, security, and high-traffic APIs.
- **Strategy:** "Ship fast, then refactor." Explicitly schedule time to address technical debt after an MVP is validated.

## Stakeholder & Team

### 4. How do you translate "Business Needs" into "Technical Solutions"?
**Answer:**
By mapping business goals (e.g., "Reduce user churn") to technical capabilities (e.g., "Implement an AI-driven personalized recommendation engine"). Use simple language to explain trade-offs to stakeholders.

### 5. Operating in a "Low-Structure" environment?
**Answer:**
Requires high self-discipline and "Engineering Maturity":
- Creating your own documentation.
- Setting up your own testing frameworks.
- Defining a clear SDLC (Branching, PRs, CI) for the team if one doesn't exist.
- Being a "Self-Starter" who doesn't wait for a ticket to be assigned.

### 6. What makes a "Senior" different from a "Mid-level" engineer in this role?
**Answer:**
The ability to see beyond the code. A senior engineer understands the business impact, identifies hidden risks, mentors others, and makes architectural decisions that save months of work in the future.
