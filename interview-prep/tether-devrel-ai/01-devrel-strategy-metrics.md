# 01 - DevRel Strategy & Metrics

## Core DevRel Strategy

### 1. What is the primary goal of Developer Relations?
**Answer:**
To build a sustainable ecosystem where developers are successful using your product. This is achieved through:
- **Education:** Content, workshops, documentation.
- **Advocacy:** Representing the community's needs to internal product teams.
- **Support:** Helping developers overcome technical hurdles.
- **Inspiration:** Showing what is possible through demos and use cases.

### 2. How do you distinguish between "Developer Marketing" and "Developer Relations"?
**Answer:**
- **Developer Marketing:** Focuses on the **Top of Funnel (Aquisition)**. Messaging, ads, and brand awareness. Goal: Get developers to sign up.
- **Developer Relations:** Focuses on **Activation, Retention, and Advocacy**. Deep technical engagement and community health. Goal: Get developers to build and stay.

### 3. Explain the "Awareness-Activation-Retention-Advocacy" funnel.
**Answer:**
- **Awareness:** Developer hears about QVAC at a conference.
- **Activation:** Developer completes their first "Hello World" local AI tutorial.
- **Retention:** Developer builds a production-grade local AI app.
- **Advocacy:** Developer writes their own blog post about why QVAC is better than cloud AI.

## Metrics & Impact

### 4. What are the key success metrics for a DevRel team?
**Answer:**
- **Adoption:** Number of active API keys or SDK installs.
- **Reach:** Content views (blog, YouTube), social media engagement.
- **Community:** GitHub stars, forks, Pull Requests, and forum activity.
- **Sentiment:** Qualitative feedback from AMAs and office hours.
- **Feedback Impact:** How many product features were directly influenced by developer requests.

### 5. How do you measure the ROI of a Developer Conference?
**Answer:**
- **Pre-event:** Number of scheduled meetings/workshops.
- **During event:** Booth traffic, demo engagement.
- **Post-event:** Spike in SDK downloads, number of new projects on GitHub, and conversion of leads to active developers.

### 6. How do you handle a "Negative Community Sentiment"?
**Answer:**
- **Acknowledge:** Don't ignore or delete negative comments.
- **Investigate:** Is it a bug, a documentation gap, or a pricing change?
- **Communicate:** Explain *why* the issue exists and what the timeline is for a fix.
- **Internal Loop:** Bring the sentiment back to the product team to prevent recurrence.
