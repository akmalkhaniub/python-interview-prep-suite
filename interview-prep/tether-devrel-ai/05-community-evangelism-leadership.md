# 05 - Community, Evangelism & Leadership

## Evangelism & Speaking

### 1. How do you explain "Local-first AI" to a non-technical audience?
**Answer:**
"Think of it like having the power of ChatGPT entirely inside your own laptop. You don't need the internet, your data stays private, and it's free to run. QVAC makes this easy for developers to build into their own apps."

### 2. Tips for a successful "Technical Live Demo"?
**Answer:**
- **Record a Backup:** Always have a video of the demo in case of hardware/network failure.
- **Start Simple:** Show the "Magic" moment in the first 30 seconds.
- **Explain the Architecture:** Use a slide to show how the components (JS -> Node -> Llama.cpp) connect.
- **Engage:** Ask the audience for a prompt or an idea to try live.

### 3. How do you scale yourself as a "Sole Advocate"?
**Answer:**
- **Automation:** Use templates for blog posts and social media.
- **Enablement:** Create "Community Heroes" who answer questions for you.
- **Content Reuse:** Turn one conference talk into a blog post, a series of tweets, and a tutorial video.

## Community & Feedback

### 4. How do you build an "Open Source Community" around a product?
**Answer:**
- **Incentivize:** Swag, recognition, or early access to features.
- **Low Barrier to Entry:** Good "Good First Issue" tags on GitHub.
- **Consistency:** Regular updates, newsletters, and AMAs.
- **Listen:** Actually implement features suggested by the community.

### 5. How do you handle "Trolls" or toxic community members?
**Answer:**
- **Code of Conduct:** Have clear rules and enforce them.
- **De-escalation:** Don't get defensive. Address facts, not emotions.
- **Moderation:** Use community moderators to keep the environment healthy.

### 6. What is the "Feedback Loop" between DevRel and Engineering?
**Answer:**
1. **Gather:** From GitHub, Discord, Twitter.
2. **Synthesize:** Group common pain points and quantify their impact.
3. **Report:** Present a prioritized list of developer needs to the Product Manager.
4. **Close the Loop:** Tell the developers when their requested feature has been launched.
